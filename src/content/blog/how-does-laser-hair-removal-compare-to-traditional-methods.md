## The drawbacks of traditional hair removal methods

Whether you shave, wax, or pluck, you might take issue with the drawbacks associated with your preferred hair removal method. Conventional hair removal methods can be hard on your skin or downright tedious. Consider their limitations:

### Shaving

Shaving is easy and (usually) painless, but it can cause a few mishaps like ingrown hairs, accidental cuts, and razor burns. Keeping a clean and sharp razor can limit these issues. Still, shaving cuts the hair off above the root so you can see the hair grow back in a matter of days.

### Waxing

Waxing can be an easy way to remove a lot of body hair in little time. Whether you do it yourself or visit a professional esthetician, waxing can be a highly uncomfortable process. The results last longer than shaving because waxing removes hair at the root, but you can expect to re-wax the same areas every six weeks or so if you want them to stay hair-free.

### Plucking

While plucking your eyebrows is within reason, plucking all of your body hair is nearly impossible. Even if you decide to pluck hair in only specific areas like your chin and jaw, plucking individual hairs from your skin is both time-consuming and painful.

## How laser hair removal works

Cutting-edge laser technology uses high-heat laser light to heat targeted hair follicles in your skin. The heat damages the follicle to prevent hair growth without damaging the healthy skin surrounding it.

- Arms

Our team creates a personalized strategy, usually recommending a series of 6-8 treatments spaced a few weeks apart. The treated skin remains hair-free indefinitely, and you can easily schedule touch-ups if or when hair returns.
