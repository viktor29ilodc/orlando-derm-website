## How does a laser remove tattoo ink?

Understanding how tattoo removal works might motivate you to try it for yourself. The way that laser tattoo removal works is fascinating: The powerful laser light breaks down ink pigment particles in your skin until they're small enough for your lymphatic system to flush them out, thus removing the tattoo.

We can adjust the laser's setting to remove all of the colors in your tattoo, which gives you the best possible removal outcome. With plenty of experience in using medical lasers, we can leave the surrounding skin unscathed while minimizing your risk of scarring and other complications.

## Planning your sessions

As we've established, most tattoos require multiple tattoo removal sessions to eliminate all of the pigment. To determine the number of sessions appropriate for your unwanted tattoo, dermatologists consider several parameters. Your tattoo might take more or fewer sessions based on:

- The depth of the ink
- The type of ink
- The size of the tattoo
- The colors in the piece
- Whether it's an amateur or professional tattoo
- The age of the tattoo

You can expect to undergo 6-12 tattoo removal sessions, or possibly more, to get rid of an entire piece. Even then, you might still see a little pigment if it's too deep in your skin. You should wait around six weeks between sessions, though you won't require any downtime after a treatment.

Because laser tattoo removal can be uncomfortable, you may also have the option of using an anesthetic to numb your skin before each session. Doing so can make sessions more tolerable, motivating you to keep up with evenly-spaced tattoo removal sessions.
