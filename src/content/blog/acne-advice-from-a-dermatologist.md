Acne is a common skin condition that affects nearly everyone at some point in their life. While it may seem like a minor nuisance, it can have a huge impact on someone's self-confidence and overall well being. To address this issue, it is important to know what causes acne, and have access to effective treatment options. Here, we provide advice from a dermatologist on how to manage acne, and tips on preventing future breakouts.

## Causes of Acne

Acne can be caused by a variety of things, including hormones, genetics, and poor hygiene. Hormonal changes in the body can cause excess sebum production, which can clog pores resulting in the formation of acne. Additionally, genetics may play a role in the severity of acne, with some people being more prone to breakouts than others. Finally, poor hygiene can contribute to acne as dirt and bacteria can exacerbate the condition.

## Tips from a Dermatologist

The best way to maintain healthy skin and reduce the risk of acne breakouts is to keep the skin clean. Dermatologists recommend washing the face twice a day with a mild cleanser to remove any oil and dirt that may be clogging pores. Additionally, it is important to avoid scrubbing the skin too vigorously, as this can cause further irritation and lead to breakouts.

Another tip to keep in mind is to use non-comedogenic products. These products are designed to be non-irritating to the skin and won't clog pores. Additionally, dermatologists recommend avoiding products with strong fragrances as they can cause further skin irritation.

## Treatment Options

If you are struggling with acne, there are a few treatment options available. Over-the-counter topical treatments, such as benzoyl peroxide and salicylic acid, can help reduce inflammation and kill bacteria. Additionally, dermatologists can prescribe oral medications or antibiotics to help clear up the skin. Finally, for severe cases, dermatologists may recommend laser treatments to reduce the appearance of acne scars.

## Preventing Acne Breakouts

The best way to prevent breakouts is to keep the skin clean. Be sure to wash the face twice a day and use non-comedogenic products. Additionally, it is important to avoid touching the face with hands or objects, as this can spread bacteria and dirt onto the skin. Finally, it is important to keep stress levels in check, as high stress can worsen acne.

Acne is a common skin condition that can be managed with the right advice and treatment options. By understanding the causes of acne, and following a few simple tips from a dermatologist, you can keep your skin healthy and prevent future breakouts.
