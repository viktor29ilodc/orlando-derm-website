If you want brighter, clearer skin for the new year, we recommend adding these three resolutions to your list:

## Apply sunscreen every day

Sunscreen is important in any weather. It doesn't matter if the forecast says sun, rain, or clouds; you should apply sunscreen every day. This includes days you plan on spending mostly indoors.

Ultraviolet (UV) rays from the sun are more powerful than you might assume, and they're a primary cause of skin aging and damage. UV radiation damages the elastin in your skin, causing it to become looser and slower to heal. It also damages cells and can cause cancer.

Not only does daily sunscreen help prevent skin cancer, but it can also help delay wrinkles and keep your skin from looking dull, dry, and discolored.

## Stop smoking

Quitting smoking has a long list of health benefits, including brighter and healthier skin. Thousands of chemicals in tobacco smoke can harm your skin, restricting its blood flow and drying it out. Smoking can lead to more wrinkles, a duller complexion, and an uneven skin tone.

If you currently smoke, even only a little, consider adding smoking cessation to your New Year's resolution list. Your skin will thank you with a youthful glow.

### Chemical peels

Light to medium chemical peels exfoliate only the outermost layers of dead and dry skin, leaving you with a fresh and bright surface. You can book light chemical peels every 2-5 weeks this year to renew your glow.

### Laser resurfacing

Laser resurfacing treatments can reduce fine lines, scars, and dullness by removing one layer of dead cells at a time. They also boost collagen production to firm your skin.
