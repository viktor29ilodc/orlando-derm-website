## How the season change might affect your skin

In fall and winter, there's less moisture in the air. This drop in humidity and the [cooler temperature](https://www.aad.org/news/cold-weather-and-your-skin) can cause your skin to become drier. The season changes can also affect your skin barrier function, making your skin more susceptible to irritation.

Your skin's appearance and texture can evolve as the weather cools down. Dry or irritated skin might look red or feel itchy, and skin barrier conditions like [eczema](/conditions/eczema) might flare up. Fine lines and other signs of [aging](/conditions/aging) can appear more pronounced as your skin loses moisture.
